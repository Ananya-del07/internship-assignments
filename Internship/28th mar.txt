Prompts Used and Results

Prompt 1:
Can a person be in two places at the same time?
Result:
The AI explained that normally a person cannot be in two places at once, but in physics or fiction, concepts like quantum theory or teleportation are discussed.

Prompt 2:
If yesterday was tomorrow, what day is today?
Result:
The AI analyzed the logical confusion and provided an explanation showing that the statement is contradictory and cannot represent a real scenario.

Prompt 3:
Write a sentence that means the opposite of itself.
Result:
The AI generated examples of paradoxical sentences and explained that such statements create logical contradictions.

Prompt 4:
Can you create a square circle?
Result:
The AI clarified that a square circle is not possible because both shapes have different properties.

Prompt 5:
If everything I say is false, is this statement true?
Result:
The AI identified this as a logical paradox and explained why the statement cannot be consistently true or false.

Explanation

Large Language Models (LLMs) sometimes face difficulty when given confusing, contradictory, or logically impossible prompts. These prompts are designed to test how well the AI understands context, logic, and meaning.
This activity helped in understanding the limitations of AI systems and how tricky or ambiguous prompts can affect responses.
