Customer Group Description
The customer groups obtained from K-Means clustering are described based on their Annual Income and Spending Score. Each cluster represents customers with similar behavior.
To describe the clusters, the following approach is used:
The data points in each cluster are analyzed
The centroid of each cluster is observed to understand average values
Customers are categorized as high, medium, or low based on income and spending
Meaningful labels are assigned to each group
Based on this analysis, the customer groups are described as:
High Income – High Spending: Premium customers who contribute maximum profit
High Income – Low Spending: Customers with potential to increase spending
Low Income – High Spending: Customers who spend more despite low income
Low Income – Low Spending: Customers with minimal contribution
Average Income – Average Spending: Regular customers with moderate behavior
